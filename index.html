a project by bailing on dystopia collective & exmilitary

The book project Bailing On Dystopia, a collection of science fiction and speculative fiction by New Yorkers and Philadelphians, is about both imagination and action. Bailing on Dystopia is about imagination because it uses fantastical modes of writing to think beyond the dystopia that so often takes over our lives, both in the worlds of creativity and in the struggles we see and experience every day. Bailing On Dystopia is about action because it is a fundraiser project, with all of its proceeds directed to a community bail fund that will pay the bail for people in New York and Philadelphia jails who cannot afford to pay. This fund will be an immediate action that we can take to move beyond the real life dystopia in NYC and Philadelphia now.

Included Authors:

Kiini Ibura Salaam is a writer, painter, and traveler from New Orleans, Louisiana. Her work is rooted in eroticism, speculative events, women’s perspectives, and artistic freedom. She has been widely published and anthologized in such publications as the Dark Matter, Mojo: Conjure Stories, and Colonize This! anthologies, as well as Essence, Utne Reader, and Ms. magazines. Her short story collection Ancient, Ancient—winner of the 2012 James Tiptree, Jr. award—contains sensual tales of the fantastic, the dark, and the magical. Her micro-essays on writing can be found at www.kiiniibura.com. Her second collection--When the World Wounds--is forthcoming from Third Man Books in 2016.

Shelley Ettinger has been an activist in the LGBTQ, anti-racist, anti-police, anti-war, and labor movements for over 40 years. She is the author of the novel Vera's Will. Her poetry and short fiction have been published in Cream City Review, Nimrod,Mississippi Review and other journals, and she is currently at work on a dys/u/topian novel. Shelley is honored to be part of this project to honor the memory of Kalief Browder by contributing to the freeing of prisoners.

Rasheedah Phillips, Esq. is the Managing Attorney of the Housing unit at Community Legal Services, a mother, writer, the creator of The AfroFuturist Affair, the co-creator of Black Quantum Futurism multidisciplinary arts collective, and a founding member of Metropolarity Queer SciFi collective. She recently independently published her first speculative fiction collection, Recurrence Plot (and Other Time Travel Tales), and an anthology of experimental essays from Black visionary writers called Black Quantum Futurism: Theory & Practice Vol. I.  As part of BQF Collective, Phillips was a 2015 Artist-in-Residence at West Philadelphia Neighborhood Time Exchange, and has exhibited and performed at Temple Contemporary, Stony Island Arts Bank in Chicago, and WORM! Rotterdam, Holland. Phillips has upcoming work appearing in the book “Keywords for Radicals: The Contested Vocabulary of Late Capitalist Struggle”  and “Unveiling Visions: The Alchemy of the Black Imagination” exhibition catalogue, and has had work published in the Temple University Political and Civil Rights Journal, Atlanta Black Star, and other publications.

Rena Zoe Branson is not sure whether she has a body or is a body. She is interested in thinking about what it means to know and describe people, including herself/ves. She is worried that all of this sounds annoying. She is a neurotic and generally compassionate person. Say hi @ rena.branson@gmail.com.

Logan Paton is a writer and layabout living in Brooklyn. He works at a video game company and has a pet leopard gecko. Emma deBeer Charno is a queer alien whose molecules have seemed to be visiting this planet for the last twenty-two years or so. They are a multi-generational New Yorker. They are a writer both of and about science fiction interested in the alien encounter, body dysphoria and dismantling, intimacy, and the surreal. Gabrielle Riviera is a writer, youth mentor, and has worked with Autostraddle.com for the last five years. Her first novel, Juliet Takes a Breath, was published by Riverdale Avenue Books this past January. Gabby's short stories and poems have been published in various anthologies and journals such as Aster(ix) , and the the Lambda-Award-winning Portland Queer: Tales from the Rose City, OMG I’m Gay, a ‘zine for queer youth, and The Best of Panic! En Vivo from the East Village. Gabby's written about feminism, kissing girls, Nicki Minaj, radical politics and falling in love with queer brown communities. She’s fostered LGBTQ youth groups, QTPOC healing spaces, and taught as a multi-media artist for arts and social justice-based organizations. She was born and raised in the Bronx.

There is an island of jails in New York, the “Greatest City on Earth,” which holds people incarcerated – people who have not been convicted of a crime. Time begins to move backwards, or perhaps multiple times simultaneously. Donald Trump is president, and he plans to build an enormous impenetrable wall surrounding the entire US and remove access to confusingly, both birth control and abortions. Work becomes primarily automatized, and those who are pushed out of work are sent to mandatory Redundancy Residence colonies. NYPD set up mobile checkpoints that appear and disappear seemingly randomly in mostly Black neighborhoods, where they arrest and demand fees for obscure and strange crimes. There are two strains of a species with strictly separated roles in society, but there is a being born who cannot fit either role and shocks the world. These are the dystopias we live, and the ones we imagine. In Bailing on Dystopia, the characters in our stories come up with ways to make change and survive within. We do the same.
